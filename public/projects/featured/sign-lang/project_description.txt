Project Title:
A Hybrid Sensor-Fusion Framework for Linguistically Complete Sign Language Recognition

Duration:
2025 - 2026

Overview:
This research was initiated during my academic period at Shibaura Institute of Technology, Japan, and later presented at ICIPROB 2026 (4th International Conference on Image Processing and Robotics - IEEE conference track), held in Sri Lanka.

The work addresses a critical challenge in Sign Language Recognition (SLR): most existing systems capture only part of the language signal. Camera-based systems capture broad motion but lose depth and struggle in difficult environments, while sensor-based systems provide precise 3D hand trajectories but miss facial and body cues that encode grammar.

Core Research Problem:
How can we design an SLR system that jointly understands:
1. Manual signals (hand shape, movement, orientation)
2. Non-manual markers (facial expression, head movement, body posture)

Without both, recognition can be lexically correct but grammatically wrong.

Proposed System:
The proposed architecture is a Hybrid Neuro-Symbolic Framework with three phases:

Phase 1 - Cross-Modal Perception
- Inputs: Leap Motion 3D hand skeleton stream + RGB camera stream
- Method: Cross-Modal Attention Transformer for dynamic multimodal fusion
- Output: Gloss-level predictions with confidence

Phase 2 - Symbolic Grammar Validation
- Method: Finite State Machine (FSM)-based grammar rule checking
- Role: Validate syntax and non-manual marker consistency, then accept/re-evaluate outputs

Phase 3 - Hybrid Translation and Refinement
- Method: Rule-based translation templates with a lightweight fallback model
- Output: Natural language sentence generation with improved grammatical consistency

Research Contribution:
- Identified major SLR research gaps in multimodal grammar-aware recognition
- Proposed a complete architecture to bridge the gap between deep learning perception and symbolic linguistic validation
- Framed an uncertainty-aware fusion direction for robust real-world deployment
